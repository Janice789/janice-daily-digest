This isn't a partnership announcement; it's a white flag in the AI infrastructure war. When the world's most profitable company starts trading ownership for electricity and GPUs, capital itself has become the bottleneck, and the entire SaaS cost structure is about to reset. Every founder pricing AI features needs to read this twice.

What Happened

Google issued equity to Berkshire Hathaway in exchange for dedicated compute capacity, signaling that demand for AI infrastructure has exceeded even Google's massive cash reserves' ability to secure supply. This marks a fundamental shift: capital, not innovation, is now the primary competitive moat in AI. The deal structure suggests compute costs will remain elevated or increase, fundamentally changing unit economics for any AI-dependent product.

Business Model Breakdown

The economics of AI features just inverted: instead of declining costs (Moore's Law), we're entering an era where compute is scarce enough that even Google trades equity for it. This means AI feature costs won't drop quickly, making efficient implementation and smart feature scoping critical for sustainable margins. Free AI features or unlimited generation models become economically suicidal.

What Makes Them Succeed

Google's willingness to dilute shareholders reveals their conviction that AI capabilities are existential—they'd rather own less of a viable company than 100% of one without competitive AI. Their unfair advantage is recognizing early that capital availability matters more than R&D budgets when infrastructure is the constraint. They're buying survival, not features.

First Principles

When infrastructure becomes the constraint, economics trumps innovation. The best product loses to the adequately-funded one with guaranteed compute access. In resource-constrained markets, capital efficiency and strategic rationing become core competencies, not nice-to-haves.

Why It Matters to You

EasyBuzz's $29-$249 pricing must account for permanently elevated AI costs. Malaysian SMEs won't accept price increases, so the arbitrage is in smart feature design: which video AI features are essential vs. nice-to-have? The competitive advantage shifts to founders who can deliver perceived AI value with minimal actual compute spend. If Google is rationing, everyone downstream will be.
